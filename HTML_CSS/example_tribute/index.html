Muhammad Yunus

Banker to the poorest of the poor

Muhammad Yunus, a name synonymous with microfinance and poverty alleviation, stands as a towering figure in the fight for economic justice. Born in Bangladesh in 1940, Yunus witnessed firsthand the struggles of the poor and envisioned a world where financial exclusion wouldn't limit potential. His revolutionary concept, Grameen Bank, challenged traditional banking norms and empowered millions, particularly women, to break free from the cycle of poverty.

Here's a time line of Muhammad Yunus's life:

  • 1940: Born in Chittagong, Bangladesh
  • 1965: Graduated from Dhaka University with a degree in economics
  • 1976: Started Grameen Bank
  • 2006: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize

"Once poverty is gone, we'll need to build museums to display its horrors to future generations. They'll wonder why poverty continued so long in human society - how a few people could live in luxury while billions dwelt in misery, deprivation and despair."
